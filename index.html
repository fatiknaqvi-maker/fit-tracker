How Grading Works

Each exercise gets a rank based on how close your reps were to the top of your rep range. Your session rank is the average across all completed exercises.

Per-Exercise Grades

S
All sets at max reps. Top of your rep range hit on every set. Time to add weight (clean S only).
A
All but one set at max. One set fell short. Push that last set up next time.
B
Average reps ≥ 85% of max. Solid working session, close to A.
C
Average reps ≥ 60% of max. You hit the work but you're at the bottom of the range. Push reps up.
D
Below 60% or sets skipped. Weight likely too heavy, or you didn't complete the work.

Half & Assisted Reps

Reps flagged as ½ (partial range) or A (assisted) count at 50%. e.g. 8 reps with ½ flag = 4 toward grading.

Session Rank

Average of every completed exercise's rank, rounded. Skipping exercises lowers it. Hit S across the board → 👑

When Weight Goes Up

Compounds (bench, squat, OHP, etc): need two clean S's in a row before bumping.
Isolation work: one clean S = bump immediately.
"Clean" means no half or assisted reps in that session.
